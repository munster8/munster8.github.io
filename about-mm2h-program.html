About the Malaysia My Second Home Program

The Malaysia My Second Home (MM2H) program, launched by the Malaysian government back in 2002, offers foreign nationals the opportunity to reside in Malaysia on a long-term basis. Designed for individuals who wish to make Malaysia their second home, the program grants qualified applicants a renewable, five-year multiple-entry social visit pass. This allows participants to enjoy the lifestyle, benefits, and natural beauty that Malaysia has to offer, while being part of a welcoming, multicultural community.

Key Benefits

  • Long-Term Stay: Successful applicants can reside in Malaysia for as long as their pass remains valid, with the option to renew every five years.

  • Multiple Entry: With a multiple-entry visa, participants have the freedom to travel in and out of Malaysia easily, making it convenient for frequent travelers and expatriates.

  • Family Inclusion: Approved participants may bring their spouses, unmarried children under 21, and parents over 60 as dependents to Malaysia under the same program, making it a family-friendly option.

  • High Quality of Life: Malaysia offers a high standard of living with access to excellent healthcare, modern infrastructure, and diverse cultural experiences, all at an affordable cost.

Recent Changes to the MM2H Program (From 2024 Onwards)

In response to evolving global and local needs, the MM2H program underwent significant updates in 2024. These changes include revised eligibility requirements, updated application fees, and new financial criteria. These adjustments aim to ensure the program's sustainability and align with Malaysia's strategic objectives, making it essential for applicants to stay informed about the latest requirements.

Requirements for Eligibility

MM2H has different requirements based on age, income, and financial stability, organized into tiers. Each tier has specific eligibility criteria and fees, tailored to meet the needs of different applicant profiles.
